## Open Decisions For The User

These do not block plans 001-002, but they should be resolved before finalizing the LLM prompt and validation criteria:

- Should theoretical papers about causal mechanisms but without empirical causal estimation be labeled `causal` or `other`?
- Should forecasting in macro/finance count as `predictive` even when the paper is primarily structural or theoretical?
- Is title-only classification acceptable for missing abstracts, or should those rows be `insufficient_text` until another source fills abstracts?
- What minimum validation performance is required before treating the 1975-2025 labels as analysis-ready? A reasonable default is at least 85% agreement on high-confidence labels and no obvious decade/journal-specific failure mode.
- Are corrections, errata, supplements, referee lists, and election-of-fellows notes always excluded from the denominator, or should any of them remain in-scope when they contain substantive research content?
- For recovery scale-up, the current working default is balanced recovery across
  journal-decade cells, with `docs/recovery_cell_targets.md` tracking the 20%
  insufficient-text target share and R001 coverage. The remaining decision is
  whether to tighten that target or accept cell-specific monitoring once the
  first R001 imports are measured.
- For ScienceOn scale-up, decide whether a route that yields at least 30%
  import-ready tier-A abstracts in a bounded batch can proceed to the next
  recovery batch, or whether it needs human review after each 25-row scan.
