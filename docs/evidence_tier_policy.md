# Evidence Tier Policy

This is the shared policy for insufficient-text recovery imports. Recovery staging, split preflight, action-progress readiness, and abstract-backfill import should all treat the same tiers as importable.

- Machine-readable policy: `outputs/tables/enriched/evidence_tier_policy.csv`
- Importable tiers: `tier_a_formal_abstract, tier_b_source_description, tier_c_first_page_abstract_or_intro`
- Non-importable tiers: `tier_d_title_only_triage, tier_e_blocked`

## Policy Table

| evidence_tier                       | short_label                 | importable | definition                                                                                                                         | allowed_use                                                                                                                             | minimum_provenance                         | reject_when                                                                                                              |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| tier_a_formal_abstract              | A formal abstract           | yes        | A source-provided article abstract from a publisher, journal page, index, DOI record, or library metadata record.                  | May be imported as classification text when source provenance is present.                                                               | source plus source_url or source_record_id | Reject if the text is title-only, citation-only, boilerplate, or not article-specific.                                   |
| tier_b_source_description           | B source description        | yes        | A source-provided article description, summary, or metadata abstract that is not explicitly labeled as a formal abstract.          | May be imported as classification text when source provenance is present and the text describes the article content.                    | source plus source_url or source_record_id | Reject if the description only repeats the title, bibliographic citation, rights text, or access instructions.           |
| tier_c_first_page_abstract_or_intro | C first-page abstract/intro | yes        | Abstract-like text, introduction opening text, or first-page summary text from a verified public article PDF or first-page source. | May be imported as classification text only when the source is public, article-specific, and not a previously blocked or suspect route. | source plus source_url or source_record_id | Reject if the PDF route is blocked, paywalled, a challenge page, a table of contents, or not clearly the target article. |
| tier_d_title_only_triage            | D title-only triage         | no         | A reviewer or automation judgment based only on title text or title-level cues.                                                    | May guide prioritization and scope triage, but must not be imported as classification text.                                             | not importable                             | Always reject at import/preflight for recovered abstract text.                                                           |
| tier_e_blocked                      | E blocked or ambiguous      | no         | Evidence is blocked, ambiguous, inaccessible, source-incomplete, or not usable for article-level classification text.              | May document why a row remains unresolved.                                                                                              | not importable                             | Always reject at import/preflight for recovered abstract text.                                                           |
